Experience

This page will recount my experiences during my stay in Wales, a journey that will forever be etched in my memory.

Where do i stayed

Around of Haverfordwest, a town which is located my collegee: Pembrokeshire College, I stayed in a village called: Wolfscastle, which i was received by Phil and Cheryl Ratcliff.

social interaction

In general, people are very polite. It is customary to use the word 'please' at the end of most sentences, especially when making requests. However, they are also quite reserved, which can make it a bit challenging to make friends. As my host, Cheryl Ratcliff, often reminded me, 'Don't give up'.

Weather

Obviously, the climate is cold, however, the Welsh region as a whole is slightly warmer, and even warmer is the Western region, where Haverfordwest is located, which is close to the country's coast. However, it still gets very cold as December approaches, meaning the closer to December, the colder it gets. A disadvantage of Haverfordwest's location near the sea is that the chances of snowfall are very low, and when it does snow, the snow is not thick enough for making snowballs or snowmen, but it is still good to post on a social media.

Education

Education here in Wales is excellent, based on my school experience. Although most of my classes were IT-focused, I also had English and math classes. These subjects incorporated technology, such as digital whiteboards, and online assignments. The school itself had modern facilities, like electronic doors. The teachers were really demanding, pushing me to improve in every assignment. They were also very understanding and patient with me, especially when I struggled with English. Overall, the teachers were excellent.
